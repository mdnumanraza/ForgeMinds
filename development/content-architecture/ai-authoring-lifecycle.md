# ForgeMinds — Content Authoring Lifecycle

> **Phase:** 2.6.6 — Authoring Lifecycle
> **Purpose:** Define the content lifecycle for every authorable entity type in ForgeMinds. Answers: "What state is this content in, who is responsible for it, and what must be true before it can move forward — or go live?"
> **Status:** v1 — planning only. No implementation, no schemas.
> **Owned by:** AI
> **Cites:** `ai-content-authoring-architecture.md` (Hybrid E3 recommendation), `ai-beat-model.md`, `ai-phase-02-02-campaign-act-stage-model.md`, `ai-phase-02-03-quest-npc-castmember-model.md`

---

## What this document covers

ForgeMinds content is authored by humans, by AI, and eventually by combinations of both. Every piece of content — a campaign skeleton, a stage, a character, a quest, a single beat — moves through a lifecycle from raw draft to playable experience and, eventually, to deprecation.

This document defines:

- The lifecycle states for each of the five authorable entity types
- Who owns what at each state, what must be true to enter and exit it, and what validation runs
- How human-authored content moves through the lifecycle
- How AI-generated content moves through the lifecycle (including the mandatory human review gate)
- What the lifecycle must support for future collaborative editing
- How a staged review process fits into the lifecycle

**Non-engineers reading this document:** the lifecycle is the set of steps that every piece of content must pass through before players can experience it. Think of it like a manuscript going from first draft to published book — except the stages are more precise and some steps are automated.

---

## Core rules (read these first)

These rules apply across all entity types and override any entity-specific guidance.

**Rule L-1 — The PLAYABLE gate is human-controlled.**
No content may reach PLAYABLE status without a human approver signing off at the REVIEW state. This applies regardless of whether the content was written by a human or generated by AI. AI-generated content enters at DRAFT and requires explicit human approval before APPROVED. There is no automated path past REVIEW.

**Rule L-2 — PLAYABLE is what the game loads.**
The Blueprint Viewer, the game engine, and all player-facing systems load only content that is in PLAYABLE state. DRAFT, REVIEW, and APPROVED content is not visible to players. This boundary is enforced by the content loader, not by the authoring tool.

**Rule L-3 — DEPRECATED is preserved, not deleted.**
Content that reaches DEPRECATED state is never deleted. It is marked inactive and excluded from new game sessions, but all save states that reference deprecated content must remain valid. A player mid-way through a deprecated stage must be able to complete it.

**Rule L-4 — Entity lifecycle floors.**
A child entity cannot be in a higher state than its parent. A Beat cannot be PLAYABLE if its Stage is still in DRAFT. A Stage cannot be PLAYABLE if its Campaign is still in REVIEW. The floor is the parent's current state. See the floor table in each entity section.

**Rule L-5 — Entity-level granularity.**
Lifecycle states apply at the individual entity level. A single Beat can be in DRAFT while its Stage is APPROVED. This supports incremental authoring — a content designer can work on new beats while the rest of a stage is already playable.

---

## Lifecycle states — canonical set

The following five states apply to all entity types, with entity-specific entry and exit criteria defined in each section. Entities that genuinely do not need a particular state are noted.

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **DRAFT** | Content exists but is incomplete or untested. Not visible to players. Not yet reviewed. |
| **REVIEW** | Content is complete enough to evaluate. Awaiting human review. Automated checks have run. |
| **APPROVED** | A human reviewer has signed off. Content is structurally and narratively sound. Not yet live. |
| **PLAYABLE** | Content is live. Players can experience it. The game engine loads it. Requires a promote decision. |
| **DEPRECATED** | Content has been retired from active use. Preserved for save-state compatibility. No new players encounter it. |

## Entity 1 — Campaign

A Campaign is the top-level content container: one technical domain, one cast, one player arc, one complete adventure.

### Lifecycle states

---

#### DRAFT

**Owner:** Content designer (human) or AI generation task (with a human author assigned as owner immediately upon creation).

**Entry criteria:**
- A campaign identifier exists (slug, title, domain declaration)
- At minimum `campaign.yaml` has been created with the required top-level fields (id, title, domain)
- The campaign has been assigned to at least one human author who accepts ownership

**Exit criteria (to REVIEW):**
- All Acts are defined with titles, learning arc phases, and ordered stage lists (stage IDs may point to DRAFT stages — they do not need to be complete yet)
- The cast roster is declared (CastMember identifiers listed — individual cast files do not need to be APPROVED yet)
- The progression model is declared (at minimum ProgressionLevel thresholds are defined)
- Campaign-scoped quests are listed (can be stubs — must be named and scoped)
- At least one Theme reference exists
- All Concept references are declared

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Schema check: required top-level fields are present and non-empty
- No ID collisions with existing campaigns on the platform
- Concept references resolve to valid platform-level Concept IDs
- Theme references resolve to valid platform-level Theme IDs

---

#### REVIEW

**Owner:** Designated lead reviewer (a human with authority to approve campaign-level decisions — narrative lead, curriculum lead, or project owner).

**Entry criteria:**
- All DRAFT exit criteria met
- AI-generated content flag: if any portion of the campaign was AI-generated, the generating author must annotate what was generated (in a review note, not in the content itself) before REVIEW is entered

**Exit criteria (to APPROVED):**
- Lead reviewer has confirmed:
  - Domain coverage is appropriate and complete across all Acts
  - The Act structure correctly represents the intended learning arc phases (Discovery → Responsibility → Mastery → Reckoning or equivalent)
  - Cast roster is appropriate for the campaign's length and emotional arc
  - Progression model is calibrated (not trivial, not punishing)
  - Campaign-scoped quests are coherent across the stages they span
- All automated validation checks pass (see below)
- Any reviewer-raised issues are resolved and re-reviewed

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- All DRAFT validations re-run
- Cross-campaign uniqueness: all CastMember IDs unique across the platform
- Stage list completeness: every stage ID declared in `campaign.yaml` has a corresponding stage file (DRAFT or higher)
- Final Stage marker exists and is valid
- Optional stage registry entries each point to a valid stage ID marked optional in its stage file
- Campaign-scoped quests span only stages declared in this campaign

---

#### APPROVED

**Owner:** Lead reviewer (responsibility shifts to the publishing coordinator for the promote-to-PLAYABLE step).

**Entry criteria:**
- REVIEW exit criteria met
- Lead reviewer has recorded sign-off (name, date, any conditions noted)

**Exit criteria (to PLAYABLE):**
- All Stages that are required for a valid play-through are in PLAYABLE state (optional stages may still be in APPROVED or lower)
- All referenced CastMembers are in PLAYABLE state
- The game engine's content loader has been pointed at this campaign's files and returns no loading errors
- A responsible party (project owner or publishing coordinator) has explicitly triggered the promote action — this is a deliberate human step, not automatic

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Cross-stage validation pass: concept prerequisites satisfied across all Acts
- Stage Portal chain forms a valid DAG (no circular references, no broken next-stage links)
- All required beat types (per Campaign's configuration) are present in every required stage

---

#### PLAYABLE

**Owner:** Content operations (ongoing). The original author remains on record but day-to-day monitoring is the responsibility of content operations or the assigned maintainer.

**Entry criteria:**
- APPROVED exit criteria met
- Promote action explicitly triggered by an authorised person

**Exit criteria (to DEPRECATED):**
- A replacement campaign exists in PLAYABLE state, or the campaign is intentionally retired
- A deprecation notice period has elapsed (duration determined by project policy — long enough for active players to complete it)
- All active save states referencing this campaign have been assessed and a compatibility plan exists (continuation path or graceful end-of-campaign message)
- An authorised person has explicitly triggered the deprecate action

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Continuous: loading health checks run at deploy time and after any content update
- No automated demotion — a PLAYABLE campaign cannot be moved back to APPROVED or lower by any automated process

---

#### DEPRECATED

**Owner:** Content archive. The deprecating author records the reason and the date.

**Entry criteria:**
- PLAYABLE exit criteria met
- Deprecate action explicitly triggered

**Exit criteria:**
- None. DEPRECATED is a terminal state. A deprecated campaign is never promoted.
- If a major revision is needed, a new campaign (new ID, new files) is created at DRAFT. The old campaign remains DEPRECATED.

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Save-state compatibility check: any save state referencing this campaign continues to resolve without errors
- No content validation required — deprecated content is frozen

---

### Lifecycle floor for Campaign

A Campaign is the root entity. It has no parent. All other entities derive their floor from it.

## Entity 2 — Stage

A Stage is the primary unit of content: one region, one concept, one complete gameplay arc. A Stage lives inside an Act inside a Campaign.

### Lifecycle states

---

#### DRAFT

**Owner:** Stage author (human or AI-generation task with a human assigned as owner).

**Entry criteria:**
- A stage file exists (`stages/stage-N.yaml`) with the required metadata fields (id, title, conceptRef, act reference)
- The stage appears in `campaign.yaml`'s act stage list
- A human author is assigned as owner

**Exit criteria (to REVIEW):**
- All required beat types (as declared by the Campaign) are present in the beat sequence
- Knowledge density target is met (minimum KnowledgeBeat count as declared by the Campaign)
- No Beat in the sequence has an empty payload (all payload fields are populated, even if rough)
- All CastMember references in AppearanceTriggers resolve to valid CastMember IDs in the campaign cast
- The Stage's Portal references a valid next-stage ID (or is marked Final Stage)
- Beat positions are unique and form a valid ordering

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Schema check: required stage fields present
- Beat sequence: position uniqueness, type validity
- Beat ordering rules BO-1 through BO-7 (at least one KNOWLEDGE before first ENCOUNTER, DUNGEON before BOSS, etc.)
- CastMember ID references resolve in `campaign.yaml`
- Portal next-stage reference resolves in `campaign.yaml`

---

#### REVIEW

**Owner:** Designated stage reviewer — either a peer content designer (human-authored stages) or the human author who owns the AI-generated content.

**Entry criteria:**
- All DRAFT exit criteria met
- AI content flag: if beats were AI-generated, the author annotates which beats and what generation prompt was used (in review notes)

**Exit criteria (to APPROVED):**
- Reviewer has confirmed:
  - The stage's narrative identity is specific and memorable (not generic)
  - Knowledge beats introduce the primary Concept clearly and sequentially
  - Quest steps cannot be completed without demonstrating understanding of the Concept
  - Encounter and Boss concepts are correctly scoped to what has been taught
  - Dialogue states for stage-local NPCs are complete (pre-quest, mid-quest, post-quest at minimum)
  - The stage feels like a coherent arc from Arrival to Portal, not a list of tasks
- All automated validation checks pass
- Any reviewer-raised issues are resolved and re-reviewed

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- All DRAFT validations re-run
- Boss concept coverage: Boss references only Concepts that appear in KnowledgeBeats in this Stage or prior Stages in the Act
- Concept completeness: every Concept referenced anywhere in this stage appears in the Campaign's Concept reference list
- AppearanceTrigger consistency: every trigger points to a valid DialogueState ID within the target CastMember's StageAppearance for this Stage

---

#### APPROVED

**Owner:** Stage author (now accountable for the content being live-ready). The reviewer's sign-off is on record.

**Entry criteria:**
- REVIEW exit criteria met
- Reviewer has recorded sign-off

**Exit criteria (to PLAYABLE):**
- The Campaign this Stage belongs to is in APPROVED or PLAYABLE state
- All CastMembers referenced by this Stage's AppearanceTriggers are in APPROVED or PLAYABLE state
- A human (stage author or publishing coordinator) has explicitly triggered the promote action

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Full stage loading test: the content loader builds the complete Stage object from the stage file and all referenced entities without errors
- Blueprint Viewer render test: the Stage renders without errors in the Campaign View and Stage View

**Lifecycle floor:**
A Stage cannot be in APPROVED if its Campaign is in DRAFT. The floor is the Campaign's state.

---

#### PLAYABLE

**Owner:** Content operations.

**Entry criteria:**
- APPROVED exit criteria met
- Promote action explicitly triggered

**Exit criteria (to DEPRECATED):**
- Stage is being replaced by a revised version (new stage file, new ID), or the parent Campaign is being deprecated
- Any active save states at this stage have a continuation plan
- An authorised person has triggered the deprecate action

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Loading health checks at deploy time
- No automated demotion

**Lifecycle floor:**
A Stage cannot be PLAYABLE if its Campaign is not PLAYABLE.

---

#### DEPRECATED

**Owner:** Content archive.

**Entry criteria:**
- PLAYABLE exit criteria met

**Exit criteria:**
- Terminal state. Revisions create a new stage at DRAFT with a new ID.

**Compatibility rule:**
Any save state recording a player's position within a deprecated Stage must remain resolvable. The Stage's beats and their IDs are preserved exactly as they were at the time of deprecation.

---

### Lifecycle floor for Stage

| Stage state | Minimum Campaign state |
|---|---|
| DRAFT | Any (Campaign may still be DRAFT) |
| REVIEW | DRAFT or higher |
| APPROVED | DRAFT or higher |
| PLAYABLE | PLAYABLE only |
| DEPRECATED | Any (parent may be DEPRECATED or PLAYABLE) |

---

## Entity 3 — Cast Member

A CastMember is a campaign-level recurring character. They appear across multiple stages and carry an arc. Their definition lives in `cast/name.yaml`. Their lifecycle is tied to the Campaign that owns them.

### Lifecycle states

---

#### DRAFT

**Owner:** CastMember author (human or AI-generation task with a human assigned). A CastMember has a single author responsible for character consistency across all StageAppearances.

**Entry criteria:**
- A cast file exists (`cast/name.yaml`) with the required definition fields (id, name, role, personality, arcSummary)
- The CastMember ID appears in `campaign.yaml`'s cast roster

**Exit criteria (to REVIEW):**
- Permanent definition is complete: name, role, personality summary, full arcSummary, theme-variant names for all Themes the Campaign supports
- At least one StageAppearance is authored with at least one DialogueState
- Every StageAppearance that is declared has at minimum a default DialogueState
- The arc beat for each StageAppearance is consistent with the arcSummary trajectory

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Schema check: required definition fields present
- Every StageAppearance references a valid Stage ID in `campaign.yaml`
- No CastMember ID collision with any other CastMember in any campaign

---

#### REVIEW

**Owner:** Narrative lead or designated character reviewer. CastMember review is a narrative-quality gate, not just a technical one — the reviewer must assess whether the character's arc is coherent and emotionally authentic.

**Entry criteria:**
- All DRAFT exit criteria met
- All StageAppearances for stages that are currently in REVIEW or higher are authored (it is acceptable for StageAppearances for not-yet-started stages to be stubs at this point)
- AI content flag: if the cast file was AI-generated, the author annotates this in review notes

**Exit criteria (to APPROVED):**
- Reviewer has confirmed:
  - The character's arc is coherent from first to last appearance
  - Personality is consistent across all StageAppearances
  - Theme-variant names and descriptions are appropriate for all Campaign themes
  - The arcSummary correctly summarises what the player will experience
  - Deliberately unresolved characters (such as Voss) sustain their intended ambiguity — the reviewer must not resolve what is meant to be unresolved
- All automated validation checks pass
- Any reviewer-raised issues are resolved and re-reviewed

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- All DRAFT validations re-run
- Every Stage referenced in a StageAppearance exists in `campaign.yaml`
- Every StageAppearance has at least one DialogueState
- Theme coverage: theme-variant content exists for every Theme the Campaign declares
- Arc consistency check (human-reviewed, no automated equivalent)

---

#### APPROVED

**Owner:** CastMember author (responsible for adding new StageAppearances as stages are added to the campaign).

**Entry criteria:**
- REVIEW exit criteria met

**Exit criteria (to PLAYABLE):**
- The Campaign is in APPROVED or PLAYABLE state
- All StageAppearances for stages that are about to go PLAYABLE are fully authored and have passed their own review
- An authorised person has explicitly triggered the promote action

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Cross-stage appearance consistency: no StageAppearance references a DialogueLine concept-gate for a Concept that has not been introduced in the Campaign by that Stage
- AppearanceTrigger linkage: every Stage that references this CastMember has a corresponding AppearanceTrigger for this character

**Lifecycle floor:**
A CastMember cannot be APPROVED if its Campaign is in DRAFT.

---

#### PLAYABLE

**Owner:** Content operations.

**Entry criteria:**
- APPROVED exit criteria met
- Promote action explicitly triggered

**Exit criteria (to DEPRECATED):**
- Campaign is being deprecated, or a revised version of the character is being introduced (rare — character revisions are usually made in-place with a content update, not a new ID)
- An authorised person has triggered the deprecate action

**Lifecycle floor:**
A CastMember cannot be PLAYABLE if its Campaign is not PLAYABLE.

---

#### DEPRECATED

**Owner:** Content archive.

**Entry criteria:**
- PLAYABLE exit criteria met

**Exit criteria:**
- Terminal state.

**Compatibility rule:**
DialogueState IDs and DialogueLine IDs referenced in save states must remain resolvable. Deprecated CastMember files are frozen.

---

### Lifecycle floor for CastMember

| CastMember state | Minimum Campaign state |
|---|---|
| DRAFT | Any |
| REVIEW | DRAFT or higher |
| APPROVED | DRAFT or higher |
| PLAYABLE | PLAYABLE only |
| DEPRECATED | Any |

**Additional floor rule:** A CastMember's individual StageAppearance is authored and reviewed as part of the CastMember lifecycle, not independently. An appearance is ready when the Stage it belongs to reaches REVIEW — the reviewer of that Stage is also responsible for confirming that the appearance content is appropriate for that stage.

## Entity 4 — Quest

A Quest is a structured learning-and-story contract. Stage-scoped quests are owned by a Stage beat. Campaign-scoped quests are owned by the Campaign. Both follow the same authoring lifecycle; their ownership level determines which parent's floor they inherit.

### Lifecycle states

---

#### DRAFT

**Owner:** Stage author (for stage-scoped quests) or campaign author (for campaign-scoped quests).

**Entry criteria:**
- Quest definition exists as a QUEST beat payload (stage-scoped) or as a campaign-level quest entry in `campaign.yaml` (campaign-scoped)
- Quest has an ID, a title, a scope declaration, and at least one QuestStep stub

**Exit criteria (to REVIEW):**
- All QuestSteps are defined with objectives and completion conditions
- QuestResolutionCondition references a valid Concept mastery check (or a valid alternative condition type)
- The NPC giver (if applicable) is a valid Stage-owned NPC or Campaign CastMember ID
- The reward is declared (references valid Campaign-level reward entities)
- At least three narrative stakes are visible: the problem being presented, the reason it matters to someone in the world, and what changes when it is resolved
- For campaign-scoped quests: all participating stages are declared and each stage file has a `referencedCampaignQuests` entry for this quest

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Schema check: required quest fields present
- QuestResolutionCondition Concept references resolve to valid platform-level Concept IDs
- NPC giver ID resolves to a valid NPC or CastMember in the appropriate scope
- For stage-scoped quests: no references to content outside this Stage
- For campaign-scoped quests: all declared participating stages exist in `campaign.yaml`

---

#### REVIEW

**Owner:** Stage reviewer (for stage-scoped quests) or narrative lead (for campaign-scoped quests). Quest review is conducted as part of the Stage review — there is no separate queue for individual quest review. The Stage reviewer evaluates quests as part of evaluating the complete Stage.

**Entry criteria:**
- All DRAFT exit criteria met

**Exit criteria (to APPROVED):**
- Reviewer has confirmed:
  - The quest cannot be completed through inventory collection or arbitrary action — demonstrating the Concept is required
  - QuestSteps apply the stage's concept to a concrete problem that a person in the world actually has
  - The failure and abandonment states (if applicable) are narratively appropriate and do not punish the player for not yet understanding
  - The reward is proportionate to the quest's learning value
  - For campaign-scoped quests: steps across stages form a coherent arc; the player's motivation to continue across multiple stages is clear
- All automated validation checks pass

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- All DRAFT validations re-run
- Concept prerequisite check: the Concept referenced in the QuestResolutionCondition must appear in a KNOWLEDGE beat earlier in the same Stage (or in a prior Stage's KNOWLEDGE beats for campaign-scoped quests)
- Reward consistency: reward XP and item values are within the range defined by the Campaign's progression model

---

#### APPROVED

**Owner:** Stage author or campaign author (same as DRAFT).

**Entry criteria:**
- REVIEW exit criteria met

**Exit criteria (to PLAYABLE):**
- The owning Stage (or Campaign, for campaign-scoped quests) is being promoted to PLAYABLE
- All NPCs and CastMembers referenced by this quest are in APPROVED or PLAYABLE state
- Promote action explicitly triggered (as part of the Stage or Campaign promote action — quests do not have independent promote actions)

**Lifecycle floor:**
A stage-scoped Quest cannot be APPROVED if its Stage is in DRAFT.
A campaign-scoped Quest cannot be APPROVED if its Campaign is in DRAFT.

---

#### PLAYABLE

**Owner:** Content operations (inherited from the owning Stage or Campaign).

**Entry criteria:**
- APPROVED exit criteria met — quests go PLAYABLE when their owning Stage or Campaign does

**Exit criteria (to DEPRECATED):**
- The owning Stage or Campaign is deprecated
- Or the quest is replaced by a revised version in the same Stage (the old quest beat is deprecated; a new beat is introduced)

**Lifecycle floor:**
A Quest cannot be PLAYABLE if its owning Stage (or Campaign, for campaign-scoped) is not PLAYABLE.

---

#### DEPRECATED

**Owner:** Content archive.

**Entry criteria:**
- PLAYABLE exit criteria met

**Exit criteria:**
- Terminal state.

**Compatibility rule:**
Quest IDs and QuestStep IDs referenced in save states must remain resolvable. Deprecated quests are frozen. Players mid-quest at the time of deprecation must be able to reach COMPLETED or ABANDONED states.

---

### Lifecycle floor for Quest

| Quest state | Stage-scoped minimum | Campaign-scoped minimum |
|---|---|---|
| DRAFT | Any | Any |
| REVIEW | Stage DRAFT or higher | Campaign DRAFT or higher |
| APPROVED | Stage DRAFT or higher | Campaign DRAFT or higher |
| PLAYABLE | Stage PLAYABLE | Campaign PLAYABLE |
| DEPRECATED | Any | Any |

## Entity 5 — Beat

A Beat is the atomic unit of stage structure. It is the most granular entity with its own lifecycle. Beats follow the most detailed lifecycle of all entities because they are where most day-to-day authoring work happens.

### Lifecycle states

---

#### DRAFT

**Owner:** Stage author (always human — Beats are the unit AI generation produces, but a human author must accept ownership of every generated Beat before it advances).

**Entry criteria:**
- Beat exists in the Stage's beat sequence with a valid position, a valid type, and at minimum a stub payload (even if the payload's fields are incomplete)
- Beat has been assigned a position unique within its Stage

**Exit criteria (to REVIEW):**
- The Beat's payload is fully populated: all required payload fields for this Beat's type are complete
- Any AppearanceTriggers on this Beat reference valid CastMember IDs and valid DialogueState IDs
- The Beat's position is consistent with beat ordering rules (the stage author must verify this, not wait for automated checks)

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- Schema check: position is a valid integer, type is a valid Beat type, payload is of the declared type
- Payload completeness: required fields for this specific payload type are present and non-empty
- AppearanceTrigger reference resolution: CastMember IDs and DialogueState IDs resolve
- Position uniqueness within the Stage

---

#### REVIEW

**Owner:** The Stage reviewer — Beats are reviewed in the context of their Stage, not individually. However, each Beat is assessed on its own merits: does this Beat do its job clearly and correctly?

**Entry criteria:**
- All DRAFT exit criteria met for this Beat
- For AI-generated Beats: the author must confirm they have read the Beat payload in full and accept it as their own content before REVIEW is entered. The reviewer sees AI-generated content annotated, but reviews it by the same standard as human-authored content.

**Exit criteria (to APPROVED):**
- Reviewer has confirmed for this Beat:
  - The Beat type is appropriate for what the Beat is doing (a KNOWLEDGE beat that is really an NPC interaction should be reclassified)
  - The payload content is complete, accurate, and consistent with the Stage's narrative and learning goals
  - Any AppearanceTriggers are correct and the triggered dialogue states match the stage moment
  - KNOWLEDGE beats: the concept is introduced clearly; the knowledge panel body is accurate
  - ENCOUNTER beats: the enemy concept matches what has been taught; challenge difficulty is appropriate
  - BOSS beats: all conceptsRequired have been introduced in earlier KNOWLEDGE beats in this Stage or Act
  - QUEST beats: the resolution condition requires demonstrating understanding, not just completing a task
- Automated validation checks pass

**Validation that runs in this state:**
- All DRAFT validations re-run
- Beat ordering rules BO-1 through BO-7 checked for the full Stage (not just this Beat in isolation — a Beat's validity depends on what surrounds it)
- BOSS beat concept coverage: every Concept in `conceptsRequired` has a KNOWLEDGE beat predecessor in this Stage or Act
- KNOWLEDGE beat concept reference: referenced Concept ID exists at platform level

---

#### APPROVED

**Owner:** Stage author.

**Entry criteria:**
- REVIEW exit criteria met

**Exit criteria (to PLAYABLE):**
- The owning Stage is being promoted to PLAYABLE
- All referenced entities (CastMember DialogueStates, Concept IDs, NPC/Quest payload entities) are in APPROVED or PLAYABLE state
- Promote action is triggered as part of the Stage promote action — Beats do not have independent promote actions

**Lifecycle floor:**
A Beat cannot be APPROVED if its Stage is in DRAFT.

---

#### PLAYABLE

**Owner:** Content operations (inherited from owning Stage).

**Entry criteria:**
- APPROVED exit criteria met — Beats go PLAYABLE when their Stage does

**Exit criteria (to DEPRECATED):**
- The owning Stage is deprecated, or this specific Beat is being replaced within a live Stage update (the old Beat is deprecated; a new Beat is introduced at the same or adjacent position)

**Lifecycle floor:**
A Beat cannot be PLAYABLE if its Stage is not PLAYABLE. A Beat cannot be PLAYABLE if its Stage is not PLAYABLE even if the Campaign is.

---

#### DEPRECATED

**Owner:** Content archive.

**Entry criteria:**
- PLAYABLE exit criteria met

**Exit criteria:**
- Terminal state.

**Compatibility rule:**
Beat IDs referenced in save states (checkpoint positions, beat-completion records) must remain resolvable. Deprecated Beats are frozen. The Stage's Beat sequence at the time of deprecation is preserved exactly.

---

### Lifecycle floor for Beat

| Beat state | Minimum Stage state | Minimum Campaign state |
|---|---|---|
| DRAFT | Any | Any |
| REVIEW | Stage DRAFT or higher | Any |
| APPROVED | Stage DRAFT or higher | Any |
| PLAYABLE | Stage PLAYABLE | Campaign PLAYABLE |
| DEPRECATED | Any | Any |

**The floor rule in plain language:** You can write and review a Beat at any time. But a Beat cannot go live until its Stage goes live, and a Stage cannot go live until its Campaign goes live. The chain flows downward; you cannot skip a level.

## Human-authored content workflow

This section describes the end-to-end path for content a human designer creates from scratch.

### Starting a new Stage (typical case)

1. The designer confirms the stage appears in `campaign.yaml`'s act stage list (or adds it there first).
2. They create the stage file: `stages/stage-N.yaml` with metadata fields filled in.
3. They author the Beat sequence inline in the stage file — drafting each Beat payload in order.
4. The stage is in DRAFT throughout this work. Automated schema checks run as they save.
5. When the Beat sequence is complete and all required beat types are present, the designer marks the stage ready for review (moves to REVIEW) and assigns a reviewer.
6. The reviewer reads the stage file, checks it in the Blueprint Viewer, and raises issues or approves.
7. Resolved issues lead to re-review. Approval moves the stage to APPROVED.
8. When the Campaign's PLAYABLE promote action is triggered, all APPROVED stages go PLAYABLE together (or a stage can be promoted individually if it is an optional content update).

### Authoring a CastMember appearance for a new Stage

1. The stage author opens `cast/name.yaml` and adds a new StageAppearance entry for the new Stage.
2. They author the DialogueStates for this appearance inline in the cast file.
3. They add AppearanceTriggers to the stage file pointing to the new DialogueState IDs.
4. The CastMember file remains in its current state (APPROVED or DRAFT) — adding a StageAppearance does not demote the CastMember. It does mean the new appearance is in DRAFT until reviewed.
5. The stage review covers the new appearance content. The stage reviewer confirms the appearance is appropriate for this stage.

### What the designer sees at each state

| State | What the designer can do |
|---|---|
| DRAFT | Edit freely. Run validation manually. View in Blueprint Viewer (preview mode). |
| REVIEW | Submit for review. Cannot make edits while under review without pulling back to DRAFT. |
| APPROVED | No edits allowed without demoting to DRAFT first. Waiting for promote decision. |
| PLAYABLE | Read-only. Any edit requires creating a new Beat or Stage (new ID) at DRAFT. |
| DEPRECATED | Read-only. |

## AI-generated content workflow

This section describes how AI-generated content enters the lifecycle and how it is handled at every stage. The rules in this section are non-negotiable.

### How AI content enters

All AI-generated content enters at DRAFT state, regardless of its apparent quality.

AI content is never automatically promoted to REVIEW. The human author assigned to own the content must:

1. Read the generated content in full
2. Make any corrections needed
3. Explicitly move the content to REVIEW by recording their acceptance of it

This step is the author saying: "I have read this and I take responsibility for it." Without this explicit action, AI content remains at DRAFT indefinitely.

### The mandatory human review gate

AI-generated content must pass through the same REVIEW → APPROVED gate as human-authored content. The reviewer sees a flag indicating AI generation, but evaluates the content by exactly the same criteria. There is no expedited review path for AI content.

AI content may never be auto-promoted past REVIEW. No pipeline, script, validation tool, or automated process may advance content from REVIEW to APPROVED or from APPROVED to PLAYABLE without a human action.

This rule exists because automated validation checks structural correctness — it cannot check narrative quality, learning appropriateness, or whether the content is actually teaching the concept it claims to teach.

### Tagging AI-generated content

When an author accepts AI-generated content and moves it to REVIEW, they add a review note (not in the content file itself) recording:
- That the content was AI-generated
- What generation prompt or task produced it
- Any edits the author made to the raw output

This record is preserved for audit purposes but does not affect the content's lifecycle progression after the author has accepted ownership.

### Common AI generation scenarios

**Scenario A — AI generates a complete Stage**
The generated stage file lands at DRAFT. The assigned human author reads the entire file, edits as needed, and accepts ownership. The stage enters REVIEW. Standard review process applies. No differences from human-authored content at this point.

**Scenario B — AI generates individual Beats**
Each generated Beat lands at DRAFT. The author reads each Beat, accepts or rewrites, and confirms ownership Beat-by-Beat before moving the Stage to REVIEW. A mix of human-authored and AI-generated Beats in the same Stage is fully supported — each Beat carries its own origin note.

**Scenario C — AI generates a Campaign skeleton**
The generated `campaign.yaml` lands at DRAFT. The author reviews structure, act shapes, stage IDs, and cast roster. They accept ownership and move to REVIEW. Stage files are created separately and begin their own lifecycle.

**Scenario D — AI generates a CastMember**
The generated cast file lands at DRAFT. The author reviews definition fields, arc summary, and any initial StageAppearances. They accept ownership and move to REVIEW. Character review is the most demanding AI content review type — reviewers must assess arc authenticity and personality consistency, not just structural validity.

### What AI content cannot do

- An AI system cannot move any content from DRAFT to REVIEW. That action belongs to a human.
- An AI system cannot approve content.
- An AI system cannot trigger a PLAYABLE promote.
- An AI system cannot deprecate content.
- An AI system cannot sign off in a reviewer field.

## Future: collaborative editing workflow

ForgeMinds will eventually support multiple human authors working on the same content simultaneously. The lifecycle must support this without becoming a coordination burden. This section defines what the lifecycle needs to handle — not how it will be built (that is a Phase 4+ engineering decision).

### Requirements the lifecycle must satisfy

**Concurrent authorship at Beat level.**
Two authors should be able to work on different Beats in the same Stage simultaneously. The lifecycle must support Beat-level lock granularity: Author A holds a write lock on Beat positions 1–5; Author B holds positions 6–11. Each author's Beats can be in different states (Author A's Beats in REVIEW while Author B's are still DRAFT).

**Stage review is a composite action.**
When a Stage enters REVIEW, all its Beats must be in REVIEW or higher. The Stage reviewer is reviewing the Stage as a complete arc. Partial reviews (reviewing some Beats but not others) are not supported — the Stage moves to REVIEW as a unit.

**CastMember appearances are the single-author exception.**
A CastMember file is always owned by one author at a time. This is intentional: character arc consistency requires a single voice making arc decisions. Collaborative editing on cast files should be sequential (Author A finishes their pass; Author B takes over), not concurrent.

**Conflict resolution for campaign.yaml.**
Because `campaign.yaml` is the one shared file touched by all Stage authors, the lifecycle must support a designated campaign.yaml owner who merges structural changes (new stage entries, new cast entries). Stage authors should not be able to directly modify `campaign.yaml` — they submit requests that the campaign owner applies.

**Review assignment in a team.**
The REVIEW state must carry an explicit reviewer assignment. In a team context, a Stage in REVIEW must name the human who is reviewing it. Content sitting in REVIEW without a named reviewer is a process gap, not a lifecycle state.

**Stage-level authorship credit.**
The lifecycle must record which humans authored or reviewed each entity. When multiple authors work on the same Stage, each author's contribution is attributed at the Beat level.

## Future: staged content review process

As content volume grows, a single human reviewer per entity is not scalable. A staged review process fits inside the existing lifecycle states as follows.

### The staged review model

The four-stage review process fits between DRAFT and APPROVED:

```
DRAFT
  → AI self-check (automated)
  → Peer review (another content author)
  → Lead review (narrative or curriculum lead)
APPROVED
```

These are sub-states within the REVIEW lifecycle state. From the outside, an entity in any of these sub-states is still in REVIEW. The staged model adds structure to the review queue without adding new top-level states.

### How each stage works

**AI self-check (automated)**
Runs immediately when a content author moves an entity from DRAFT to REVIEW. It is the automated validation pass: schema correctness, ordering rules, reference resolution, concept prerequisite checks. Content that fails the AI self-check is returned to DRAFT automatically — the author receives a specific list of failures to fix. Only content that passes the AI self-check advances to peer review.

This step is fully automated. No human is involved.

**Peer review**
A content author at the same level reviews the content for quality, accuracy, and narrative coherence. The peer reviewer is not the entity's own author. Peer reviewers use the Blueprint Viewer to see the content in context.

The peer reviewer has two actions: request changes (returns to the author, stays in REVIEW sub-state) or pass to lead review. The peer reviewer cannot approve directly — that authority belongs to the lead reviewer.

**Lead review**
The narrative lead, curriculum lead, or project owner reviews the content. This is the final human gate before APPROVED. The lead reviewer has authority to approve, request changes, or reject and return to DRAFT.

For AI-generated content: the lead reviewer sees the AI-generation flag and the author's acceptance note. The lead reviewer's approval is the final act that removes the AI-content flag from the entity's status — once approved, AI-generated and human-authored content are equivalent in the system.

### What this means for the lifecycle

- REVIEW becomes a structured queue, not a single waiting state
- The REVIEW → APPROVED gate is still a human action (the lead reviewer's approval)
- The AI self-check sub-state is the automated first filter, but it does not approve — it only gates access to human review
- A content author whose content passes AI self-check but fails peer review is not penalised — they are given feedback and the opportunity to revise within REVIEW without returning all the way to DRAFT

## Lifecycle interaction matrix

How entity lifecycle states interact when moving parent entities.

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| Campaign promoted to PLAYABLE | All APPROVED Stages in the Campaign go PLAYABLE. DRAFT/REVIEW Stages remain at their state (not promoted). |
| Campaign deprecated | All PLAYABLE Stages, CastMembers, and their Beats are flagged DEPRECATED. All content is frozen. |
| Stage promoted to PLAYABLE | All APPROVED Beats in the Stage go PLAYABLE. All APPROVED stage-scoped Quests go PLAYABLE. |
| Stage deprecated | All PLAYABLE Beats and stage-scoped Quests go DEPRECATED. Save states referencing them must remain valid. |
| Beat deprecated (individual) | The Beat is frozen. The Stage it belongs to is not demoted. The player must not encounter this Beat. The Stage's beat sequence is updated to mark the Beat as deprecated — the engine skips it. |
| CastMember promoted to PLAYABLE | All APPROVED StageAppearances go PLAYABLE alongside the CastMember. |
| Stage added to PLAYABLE Campaign | The new Stage follows the standard lifecycle from DRAFT. It does not go PLAYABLE just because the Campaign is PLAYABLE — it must pass its own REVIEW and APPROVED states, then be explicitly promoted. |
