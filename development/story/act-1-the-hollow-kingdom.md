# Act One: The Hollow Kingdom

> *A stranger arrives. A kingdom waits. Something enormous is broken — and no one left alive knows how to fix it.*

---

## 🌑 The World Before

Once, this kingdom hummed.

Every home was lived in. Every road carried trade. Every part of the realm knew where to find every other part — and the whole thing held together the way a well-made thing holds together: not because nothing ever went wrong, but because it was built to recover when something did.

Then the dragon came.

His name was Khaosynth. He was not what kingdoms expect from dragons — not hunger, not fire for the sake of it. He was something colder and stranger: a believer. He had watched systems fail. He had watched kingdoms collapse. He had watched people suffer because something broke that was not supposed to break, and he had arrived at a conclusion he held with absolute certainty.

*If nothing can fail, nothing will.*

So he did not burn the kingdom. He unmade it.

He cracked open the shells that held life in its smallest, most portable form — the units that let the kingdom replicate itself, move itself, sustain itself anywhere. Without them, nothing could be contained. Nothing could grow from reliable seed.

He scattered the homes. A home here was not just walls and a roof. It was a living unit — a place where the smallest parts of the kingdom's work could shelter together, share what they needed, and be found. When Khaosynth scattered them, the families inside were still alive. They simply could not reach each other anymore.

He burned the orders. Not the soldiers — the mandates that told the army how many should stand, where to stand, and what to do when one fell. Without those orders, soldiers wandered leaderless. When one fell, no one came to take their place.

He silenced the crossroads. The kingdom's directory — the great record of who lived where, how to find them, how to reach the healer and the merchant and the guard — went dark. The roads still existed. They led nowhere.

By the time Khaosynth finished, the kingdom looked almost intact.

That was the cruelest part. It was not rubble. It was a place full of people who could not find each other.

---

## 🌿 The Arrival

You came knowing nothing.

That was not a weakness. It turned out to be the only credential that mattered.

Lyra found you at the edge of the kingdom's outermost fields — scorched and still, the ground hollow where it should have been full. She talked too fast. She corrected herself twice in the first minute. She explained the kingdom's wound in three sentences and then immediately complicated all three.

She was the last archivist. She had survived because she was fast and lucky and consumed by guilt over every scroll she had not saved. She believed, with the specific confidence of someone who has studied a problem for a long time, that she knew how to fix what was broken.

She was partly right. The rest she would have to learn alongside you.

---

## 🔥 What You Found

The outermost fields were hollow because the shells that once held life had been cracked open and emptied. You learned to tell the difference between a shell that was intact and one that only looked that way — the ones that flickered, almost real, never quite.

You walked north into a village where people were still alive and could not find each other. A girl named Mira sat outside a home that had stopped working. Her mother was a healer. The two of them were close enough to touch and could not communicate. Mira was patient in the way children are patient when they don't understand that waiting might not be enough.

You fixed the home.

It was a small thing. Mira looked at you the way someone looks at a person who has done something they did not know was possible. She remembered it. She would remember it for a long time.

North of the village, you found the ruins of the kingdom's army — not destroyed, just leaderless. The orders that told the force how large it should be, and what to do when someone fell, had been torn away. A soldier named Kestran had been trying to hold it together by hand, sending runners to check on each individual post, replacing the fallen one at a time. He was exhausted. He looked at you with the assessment of someone who has watched too many people arrive promising to help.

He did not speak to you for a long time. When he finally opened the gate, it was not warmth. It was something rarer: a decision that you had earned the next step.

At the crossroads — the old heart of the kingdom's communications — you found the dragon's first lieutenant. A creature that had made its home in the silenced directory and was actively keeping the kingdom's parts from finding each other. It had been placed there deliberately. Not corruption. Strategy.

You defeated it.

---

## 🌱 What Changed

The crossroads' directory began to rebuild itself.

A messenger who had been trying to deliver a letter for three days finally reached the person he was looking for. It was a small, absurd, deeply satisfying moment. Lyra laughed — real, surprised laughter.

Regions that had been silent since the attack began to transmit again.

Kestran gave you a direct order for the first time. He did not say please. It was the closest thing to approval he had shown, and it meant more than he would ever say out loud.

The kingdom was not healed. Its outer wounds were sealed. Its parts could find each other.

That was enough — for now — to move forward.

Somewhere deeper in the kingdom, in regions still dark, something older and more stubborn waited.

---

*The crossroads are open. The path splits. The interior of the kingdom calls — and it is stranger, and more wounded, than anything you have seen so far.*
