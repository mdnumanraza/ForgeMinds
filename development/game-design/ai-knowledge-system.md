# Knowledge System

> **Purpose:** How real Kubernetes concepts are introduced, practiced, and verified through play.
> **Status:** Preliminary stub — not yet finalized. Real design happens in Milestone 01 (Game Discovery) and Milestone 03 (Design Decisions).
> **Owned by:** AI

## Preliminary description

The knowledge system is the heart of what makes ForgeMinds different from a generic RPG and what makes it different from a quiz app — it is the layer that turns Kubernetes concepts into living elements of the world rather than facts to memorize. We will decide how concepts are introduced (through environment, dialogue, encounter, or all three), how mastery is practiced (through encounter mechanics, puzzles, or applied side quests), and how mastery is verified in a way that feels diegetic rather than evaluative. Candidates include concept-as-power (knowing a concept literally grants an in-game ability), concept-as-key (concepts unlock regions or NPCs), and concept-as-tool (concepts shape how the player solves later problems). A core tension we will need to face is rigor: real Kubernetes understanding must transfer to real-world work, but the game cannot devolve into a configuration test. The system also has to handle the returning-expert player who already knows the material and the under-prepared player who needs more reps, without making either feel punished.

## Open questions

- How is a concept "introduced" — environmentally, through dialogue, through challenge?
- What does "mastery" look like, and how is it detected in play?
- Should already-known concepts be skippable, and how?
- How tightly should in-game representation map to real Kubernetes mechanics?
- How do we verify learning transfers without resorting to quiz-style checks?
