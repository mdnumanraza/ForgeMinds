# Boss System

> **Purpose:** How bosses gate progress and deliver narrative + knowledge payoffs.
> **Status:** Preliminary stub — not yet finalized. Real design happens in Milestone 01 (Game Discovery) and Milestone 03 (Design Decisions).
> **Owned by:** AI

## Preliminary description

Bosses are the punctuation marks of Kubernetes Kingdom — the moments where the player proves they have internalized a stage's concept, where the story lands a beat, and where the world visibly changes after victory. We will decide whether bosses are unlocked by story progress, by knowledge mastery checks, or by some combination, and whether each boss is a unique set-piece (Zelda-style) or part of a recurring rival pattern (Pokémon-style gym leaders). Candidates include single-encounter bosses tied to each stage's concept, recurring rival figures who escalate alongside the player, and a final-act antagonist who recontextualizes the campaign's themes. A boss should feel earned: telegraphed by the stage's quests, enemies, and dialogue, then resolved in a way that gives the player both a mechanical reward and a felt understanding. We will also need to decide how failure is handled — retries, hints, or alternate paths — without breaking dramatic tension.

## Open questions

- Are bosses gated by story, by knowledge mastery, or both?
- One unique boss per stage, or a smaller cast of recurring antagonists?
- What does "defeating a boss" mean mechanically — combat, puzzle, configuration?
- How does failure work without deflating the encounter?
- What is the visible world-change after a boss falls?
