# Reward System

> **Purpose:** What players earn, why it matters, and how rewards reinforce learning.
> **Status:** Preliminary stub — not yet finalized. Real design happens in Milestone 01 (Game Discovery) and Milestone 03 (Design Decisions).
> **Owned by:** AI

## Preliminary description

Rewards are how the game tells the player "that mattered" — and in ForgeMinds, what counts as a reward is unusually loaded because it has to satisfy both RPG instincts (loot, XP, new gear) and the deeper goal of reinforcing a concept just learned. We will decide whether rewards are primarily mechanical (items, stat boosts, abilities), narrative (lore drops, world changes, character bonds), or knowledge-shaped (a concept "card" added to a codex that is also a usable tool). Candidates include classic JRPG drops, Zelda-style heart-piece equivalents tied to optional content, and bespoke rewards where the act of earning the reward IS the demonstration of mastery. A failure mode to avoid is hollow rewards — XP-bar-only feedback or generic currencies that mean nothing — because those train the player to optimize numbers rather than internalize concepts. We also need to think about cadence: too many rewards numb the player, too few make exploration feel unrewarded.

## Open questions

- What kinds of rewards exist (items, abilities, lore, knowledge-tools)?
- Is there a single currency, multiple, or none at all?
- How do we avoid hollow XP-bar feedback?
- How does a reward visibly tie back to the concept it celebrates?
- What is the cadence of rewards across a typical session?
