# Progression System

> **Purpose:** How the player grows in capability and knowledge across the campaign.
> **Status:** Preliminary stub — not yet finalized. Real design happens in Milestone 01 (Game Discovery) and Milestone 03 (Design Decisions).
> **Owned by:** AI

## Preliminary description

Progression is the spine that connects every other system — it is what turns a set of encounters into a journey. We will decide whether progression is primarily mechanical (stats, skills, gear in the JRPG tradition), primarily knowledge-based (concepts unlocked become tools), or a deliberate fusion where understanding a concept literally grants a capability in-world. Candidates include classic XP-and-level curves, Zelda-style ability unlocks tied to dungeons, and Golden Sun-style elemental kits where new "elements" map to new Kubernetes concepts. A core tension we will need to resolve: should two players ending the campaign have the same toolkit, or can builds diverge? This stub also flags that progression needs to be legible — the player should always feel they are getting stronger and smarter, and should be able to point to why. Pacing across the ~12–15 stages of Kubernetes Kingdom must avoid both early drowning and late-game power plateaus.

## Open questions

- Is progression linear, branching, or gated by knowledge checks?
- Do levels/stats exist, or is growth expressed only through unlocks?
- How is "knowledge mastery" measured in a way that feels game-like, not test-like?
- Can a player skip a concept they already know, and what does that look like?
- How do we keep mid-campaign progression from feeling like a treadmill?
