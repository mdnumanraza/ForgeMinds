# Campaign Structure

> **Purpose:** How Kubernetes Kingdom's ~12–15 stages are arranged into a cohesive journey.
> **Status:** Preliminary stub — not yet finalized. Real design happens in Milestone 01 (Game Discovery) and Milestone 03 (Design Decisions).
> **Owned by:** AI

## Preliminary description

Campaign structure is the macro-shape of Kubernetes Kingdom — how its stages connect, how they sequence concepts, and how they build narrative momentum across roughly twelve to fifteen distinct beats. We will decide whether the world is a connected overworld (Zelda-like), a route-and-town chain (Pokémon-like), or a hub-and-spoke structure where a central location unlocks new regions over time. Candidates for the underlying skeleton include a strict linear path with optional side regions, a soft-gated open structure where a few stages are reachable in any order, and an act-based arc with clear narrative inflection points. The structure must also do double duty as a curriculum: each stage carries a thematic concept (e.g., Pods, Services, Deployments, Scheduling) and the order should respect prerequisites without feeling like a syllabus. Theming pivots — Fantasy Kingdom vs. Space Galaxy — must apply to the same skeletal structure without rewrites.

## Open questions

- Is the campaign linear, semi-open, or hub-based?
- How are concept prerequisites encoded in the world map?
- Where do narrative act-breaks fall, and what marks them?
- How many side stages or optional regions should we plan for?
- Can the same structure host both Fantasy and Space themings without distortion?
